Malik Ahmed is a generative artist and healthcare technology expert from London, England. Previously, Malik worked at The Royal Marsden Hospital - the world’s leading cancer research hospital, in Chelsea, London, after graduating Pharmacy School in 2021 and ranking high in the national exam. As a self-taught programmer of 3.5 years, his Master’s thesis was on a drug informatics AI he wrote and open-sourced, called OpenPIL. The AI has been featured on the front pages of Hacker News, and won the prestigious Making A Difference Award 2021 from the University of Manchester. He is currently an AI advisor to a Y Combinator drug informatics startup.

In April 2022, Malik launched his first commercial health technology startup, Dosewolf, a medication tracker application he built over three years. The iOS app was quickly noticed by Apple, and Malik was invited to Apple Headquarters in Mayfair, London by the App Store Editorial Team to introduce Dosewolf. It has since been featured on the App Store in the U.K. under New and Trending, and is also set to be featured on the main ‘Today’ page in July-August this year for South Asian Heritage Month. Most recently, on 21 June 2022, Dosewolf was featured near the top of the Today Tab, under "New Apps We Love", and notice was recieved from the App Store Team that Dosewolf may be featured as "App of the Day" soon.

Currently, Malik is undertaking a research project in Madagascar, Africa, for his non-profit, OpenPIL, to explore the potential for healthcare technology in developing countries. It was on this journey that he learnt about the infamous Baobab trees, and was inspired to create his first public generative art collection, The Baobab.
